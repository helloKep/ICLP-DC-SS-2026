\section{Background}\label{sec:background}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Research into railway operations began as early as the 1950s and 1960s, when such papers as \textit{A Model for the Optimal Programming of Railway Freight Train Movements} in 1956 \cite{chamil56a} and \textit{Timetables for a Suburban Rail Transit System} in 1969 \cite{salzborn69a} sought to formalize problems in the domain by appealing to fields such as management science and linear programming.
	\item For the rest of the twentieth century, the railway operations literature grew as researchers explored solutions involving dynamic programming and branch and bound procedures \cite{petmer81a}, integer programming and heuristics \cite{caigoh94a}, Lagrangian relaxation (cite: Lindberg,  Nou, Nilsson), among others
